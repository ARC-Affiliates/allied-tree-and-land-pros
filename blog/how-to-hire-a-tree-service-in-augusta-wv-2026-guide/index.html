Do You Need a Permit to Cut Down a Tree in Augusta, WV?

A tree permit is written authorization from a local or state agency required before removal in regulated zones.

On private residential property in Augusta, WV, you generally do not need a permit — but trees within US-50 or WV-28 right-of-way, floodplains, or subdivision HOAs often do.

West Virginia does not regulate private-property tree removal at the state level. However, under WV Code §17-16, trees in state highway right-of-way fall under WVDOT jurisdiction. If your tree overhangs US-50 near the Augusta post office or the Capon Bridge approach, call the WVDOH Hampshire County office before cutting.

Can You Get Trees Removed for Free Near Augusta?

Free tree removal is work performed at no cost, typically by firewood cutters or utility companies in exchange for the wood or right-of-way access.

Sometimes — if the tree is healthy, accessible, and valuable as sawlog timber or firewood, local loggers and firewood processors may take it at no charge.

Credentials a legitimate Augusta tree service should carry

    • WV Contractor License — issued by the WV Division of Labor Contractor Licensing Board for jobs over $2,500.

    • General liability insurance — minimum $1,000,000 per occurrence is industry standard.

    • Workers' compensation — verify an active policy number, not just a certificate.

    • ISA-Certified Arborist (certified by the International Society of Arboriculture — isa-arbor.com).

    • TCIA accreditation — optional but a strong safety-culture signal.

Pre-hire verification checklist

    • Request the WV contractor license number and confirm it at labor.wv.gov.

    • Ask for a current insurance certificate emailed directly from the insurer.

    • Get at least three written, itemized estimates.

    • Confirm whether stump grinding and debris haul-off are included.

    • Ask who owns the wood after removal.
