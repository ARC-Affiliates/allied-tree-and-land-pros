How Do I Prepare My Yard for Tree Service?

Yard preparation reduces job time, prevents damage claims, and can lower your final bill 5-10%.

Clear vehicles, patio furniture, and pets from the work zone, then mark sprinkler heads and septic lids with flags before crews arrive.

  1. Move all vehicles at least 75 feet from the drop zone the night before.
  2. Remove patio furniture, grills, planters, and decorative items within 50 feet of the tree.
  3. Flag sprinkler heads, septic tank lids, well caps, and buried propane lines.
  4. Confine pets indoors — chainsaws and chippers run continuously for 4-8 hours.
  5. Notify neighbors if equipment will stage in shared driveways or alleys.
  6. Call 811 (West Virginia One Call) at least 48 hours ahead for any stump grinding.
  7. Photograph the yard before work begins as a damage baseline.
  8. Confirm the contractor's WV license and certificate of insurance in writing.

How Do You Estimate a Tree Service Job?

Tree service estimates are built from height, DBH, hazard rating, debris volume, and travel distance to the job site.

A reliable estimate requires an on-site visual inspection — phone quotes are non-binding and often inaccurate by 30-50%.

Experts at Allied Tree and Land Pros build estimates using five inputs: tree height (measured by clinometer), DBH (diameter at breast height, measured 4.5 feet up the trunk), hazard score (1-5 based on lean, decay, and target proximity), debris cubic yardage, and round-trip mileage from the Augusta shop. A typical residential estimate near Romney takes 20-30 minutes on site. Compare this to a phone quote, which skips the hazard assessment entirely. On-site vs phone estimates: on-site is accurate because the arborist sees decay, lean, and access. Phone is fast but unreliable because it relies on the homeowner's height guess, which averages 35% low. Always get three written estimates for jobs over $1,000.
