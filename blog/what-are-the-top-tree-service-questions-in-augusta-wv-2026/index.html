#Myths vs. facts about tree service in Augusta, WV

Myth: Topping a tree makes it safer in storms.

Fact: Topping causes weak regrowth and is explicitly discouraged by the International Society of Arboriculture; proper crown reduction is the safer alternative.

Myth: All tree service companies carry insurance.

Fact: Many uncertified crews operate without workers' compensation, leaving homeowners liable for on-site injuries.

Myth: Removing a tree is always cheaper than ongoing maintenance.

Fact: Mature trees add 7–19% to property value per USDA Forest Service research; selective pruning often preserves that value.

Myth: Tree removal in Augusta WV requires a permit.

Fact: Most private-property residential removal in Hampshire County does not require a permit unless within a state right-of-way or regulated stream buffer.

Tree removal vs. tree pruning: removal is the right choice when the tree is structurally compromised, dead, or threatening a structure, because the safety risk outweighs replacement cost. Pruning is the better tradeoff when the tree is healthy but overgrown, because retaining mature canopy preserves property value and storm-shade benefits that take decades to regrow. As of 2026, Allied Tree and Land Pros sees roughly a 60/40 split between pruning and removal calls across the Augusta service area.
