What Is the Step-by-Step Process for Hiring a Tree Trimmer?

The hiring process is the sequence of evaluation, contracting, and oversight steps a property owner follows from first call through final cleanup.

The process runs from on-site estimate to final walkthrough across roughly 1-3 weeks, depending on crew availability and weather.

  1. Step 1: On-site estimate — A certified arborist walks the property, identifies hazards, and documents tree species and DBH (diameter at breast height).
  2. Step 2: Written scope-of-work — You receive a line-item proposal referencing ANSI A300 pruning standards, debris disposal, and stump treatment.
  3. Step 3: Insurance verification — Request a COI sent directly from the insurer, not a photocopy.
  4. Step 4: Contract signing — Sign with deposit limits capped at 10-25% per WV consumer protection norms.
  5. Step 5: Work day — Crew sets up drop zones, posts signage, and follows OSHA 1910.269 line-clearance rules where applicable.
  6. Step 6: Final walkthrough — Inspect cuts at the branch collar, confirm debris removal, and release final payment.

Pre-hire verification checklist

  1. Confirm ISA certification number on treesaregood.org.
  2. Request and review a current Certificate of Insurance.
  3. Verify WV business registration via SOS online search.
  4. Read at least 10 Google reviews dated within the last 12 months.
  5. Get three written bids with line-item scope.
  6. Confirm ANSI A300 language appears in the contract.
  7. Cap deposit at 25% or less of total job cost.
  8. Photograph trees before work begins for your records.
