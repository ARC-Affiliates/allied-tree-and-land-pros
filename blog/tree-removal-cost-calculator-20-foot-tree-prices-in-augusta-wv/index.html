Can Seniors Get Trees Cut Down for Free?

Free senior tree removal programs are nonprofit or government services that remove hazardous trees at no cost for income-qualifying older adults. Several options exist for Augusta-area seniors in 2026.

Yes — seniors may qualify for free removal through WV state programs, USDA grants, or local nonprofits.

Three legitimate paths to free or reduced-cost removal:

  • USDA Emergency Forest Restoration Program — covers storm-damaged trees on rural land (source: USDA Farm Service Agency)
  • WV Division of Forestry assistance — limited cost-share for hazard trees (wvforestry.com)
  • Hampshire County Senior Services — referrals for low-income seniors needing safety-related yard work

Pricing comparison: Paid removal vs. free programs. Paid removal is faster because crews schedule within days and handle any tree. Free programs are slower because they require eligibility paperwork, are limited to verified hazards, and have waitlists of 30–90 days.

Myths vs Facts

Myth: All 20-foot trees cost the same to remove.

Fact: Location, species, and access can triple the price of identical-height trees.

Myth: Tree services will haul wood away for free.

Fact: Some include haul-off; many charge $50–$100 for disposal.

Myth: Stump grinding is included in removal.

Fact: Stump grinding is almost always quoted separately at $75–$150 per stump.

Myth: The lowest bid is always the best deal.

Fact: Uninsured low bidders shift liability to the homeowner if a worker is injured.
